Jóhann Vignir is a composer, music producer and recording engineer from Iceland currently based in Stockholm, Sweden. Over the last 14 years Jóhann has been working professionally creating and recording music along with working on post production for commercials, TV and film. With a background in garage rock and pop, Jóhann discovered a passion for contemporary music and electroacoustic music after relocating to Sweden in 2018. Jóhann has studied composition at Gotland School of Composition and the Royal Academy of Music in Stockholm. His compositional work aims at parsing out meaning from the meaningless, working with algorithms in Supercollider both spectrally and tonally. Joining these with acoustic instruments is of special interest. Jóhann’s music has been performed by Uppsala Chamber Soloists and Gotlandsmusiken and Jóhann has performed electronically at the Norberg Festival and at select concerts at the Royal Academy, both solo and accompanying other musicians . Alongside composition work Jóhann runs Fjúk (pronounced ‘fugue‘), a production company and music label, aimed at recording and releasing contemporary art music. Fjúk has collaborated with artists such as Dror Feiler, Hans-Ola Ericsson and Jörgen Petterson, also festivals/ concert series including Svensk Musikvår, Ljudvågor in Gotland and A Sudden Point of Balance.
